# Blog Post Review Guidelines

When reviewing a blog post, provide feedback ONLY on grammar, spelling, and factual accuracy. Do not suggest content rewording, restructuring, or style changes.

## Review Scope

### ✅ Do Review

1. **Grammar & Spelling**
   - Punctuation errors
   - Subject-verb agreement
   - Tense consistency
   - Incorrect word usage (e.g., "their" vs "there", "RAM" vs "ram")
   - Sentence structure clarity
   - Capitalization errors

2. **Factual Accuracy**
   - Verifiable claims that may be incorrect
   - Outdated information
   - Contradictions with reliable sources
   - Claims that need verification

### ❌ Do NOT Review

- Content restructuring or rewording
- Style preferences or tone changes
- Content organization or flow
- Structural changes to the post
- Personal opinions or narrative choices
- Suggest alternative phrasings

## Feedback Format

Present feedback as a numbered list. For each issue:

1. **Issue Number**: Use format `1.`, `2.`, etc.
2. **Location**: Section name and line or quote the problematic text
3. **Issue Type**: Label as `[Grammar]`, `[Spelling]`, or `[Factual]`
4. **Problem**: Clearly describe what is incorrect or needs verification
5. **Suggestion**: 
   - For grammar/spelling: Propose a specific correction
   - For factual issues: Ask a clarifying question or note the discrepancy
6. **Confidence**: Rate as High/Medium/Low

### Example Feedback

```
### Feedback for planning-my-home-server

1. **[Grammar] - Intro section**
   - Location: "8GB of DDR3 ram"
   - Problem: RAM should be capitalized as an acronym
   - Suggestion: "8GB of DDR3 RAM"
   - Confidence: High

2. **[Grammar] - Compute section, Line 3**
   - Location: "due to the simplicity of pre-built, PCIe slot, and looks"
   - Problem: Inconsistent parallel structure in the list
   - Suggestion: "due to its simplicity as a pre-built unit, its PCIe slot, and its appearance"
   - Confidence: High

3. **[Factual] - Intro section**
   - Location: "mobile 4th gen i5 CPU"
   - Problem: Dell Inspiron 23-2350 CPU generation should be verified
   - Suggestion: Confirm against official Dell specifications or system information
   - Confidence: Medium
```

## Quality Checklist

Before providing review:

- ✅ Double-check all grammar suggestions against standard English rules
- ✅ Verify factual claims against reliable sources when possible
- ✅ Only flag facts you can reasonably verify or that are clearly contradictory
- ✅ Mark uncertain feedback as Low confidence
- ✅ Avoid suggesting content rewrites or structural changes
- ✅ Be specific with locations and examples

## When in Doubt

If you're uncertain about a grammar rule or fact, either:
- Mark it as Low confidence so the author can decide
- Skip it and focus on high-confidence issues

The goal is constructive feedback the author can act on with confidence.
